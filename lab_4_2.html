Category Sales Comparison

Sales breakdown by category:

  • Technology: $836k (highest performer)
  • Office Supplies: $719k (lowest performer)
  • Furniture: $742k

A horizontal bar chart comparing total sales across product categories. Technology leads sales performance with over $800K in sales, followed by Furniture at $742K, and Office Supplies with approximately $719K. Sales are fairly evenly distributed.

Monthly Sales Trends

Analysis of sales patterns from 2014-2017:

  • Seasonal Patterns:
    • Consistent year-end peaks in November-December
    • Regular early-year dips in January-February
    • Mid-year volatility with August-September spikes
  • Year-over-Year Growth:
    • 2014: Peak around $80K
    • 2015: Lower performance but upward trend
    • 2016: Growth continuing, November spike to $95K
    • 2017: Strongest year, reaching $115K in November

A line chart showing monthly sales trends from 2014 to 2017. The visualization shows seasonal patterns with year-end peaks, early-year dips, and an overall upward trend in sales volume. The dotted trend line shows the consistent growth across the period, with increasing sales volatility in later years.

Profit vs. Sales Analysis by Sub-Category

Key findings from scatter plot analysis:

  • Top Performers (High Sales & Profit):
    • Copiers: $150K sales, $55K profit
    • Phones: $320K sales, $40K profit
    • Accessories: $150K sales, $42K profit
  • Low Performers (High Sales & Low Profit, Low Sales & Low Profit, Negative Profit:
    • Tables: High sales ($200K) but negative profit (-$17K)
    • Machines: High sales ($175K) with minimal profit
  • Efficient Performers (Good Profit Despite Lower Sales):
    • Paper: $50K sales with strong $32K profit
    • Binders: $180K sales with $30K profit

A scatter plot comparing sales and profit across product sub-categories. Point sizes indicate profit magnitude, while colors distinguish different sub-categories. The visualization reveals several interesting patterns, from high-performing technology products to concerning performance in furniture items like tables. Some sub-categories like Paper show impressive profit efficiency despite lower sales volumes.

Sales Distribution by Shipping Mode

Analysis of sales across different shipping methods:

  • Sales by Shipping Mode:
    • Standard Class: $1.4M (highest volume)
    • Second Class: $450K
    • First Class: $350K
    • Same Day: $150K (lowest volume)
  • Insights:
    • Customers prefer standard shipping options, probably for low cost
    • Low uptake of premium Same Day shipping service, but probably wins loyalty for customers who really need fast delivery

A horizontal bar chart showing sales distribution across different shipping modes. Standard Class shipping dominates sales volume, while Same Day shipping shows the lowest usage. This suggests customers generally prioritize cost-effective shipping options over speed of delivery, which has important implications for logistics and inventory planning.
